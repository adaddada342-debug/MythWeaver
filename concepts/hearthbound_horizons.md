# Hearthbound Horizons

## Pack Identity

Hearthbound Horizons is a polished Fabric 1.20.1 survival modpack about slowly building a beautiful
life in a world that feels worth staying in. The experience is relaxed, atmospheric, scenic, and
progression-based without becoming grindy or technical.

The player should feel like they are living in the world over many in-game weeks: building a warm
home, improving villages, cooking better meals, creating roads and bridges, decorating interiors,
mapping distant regions, and returning from long journeys with meaningful materials and stories.

## Core Fantasy

Settle into a beautiful landscape and make it home. Explore forests, mountains, oceans, caves,
villages, and ruins at a comfortable pace. Improve your base, food, storage, workshop, travel
network, and settlement over time. The world should be alive, comforting, and visually rewarding.

## Tone

- Relaxing
- Atmospheric
- Immersive
- Beautiful
- Cozy but not empty
- Rewarding without pressure
- Occasional danger, never constant stress

## Anti-Goals

- No kitchen sink bloat.
- No chaotic power fantasy.
- No hardcore survival realism.
- No giant tech trees.
- No boss-heavy RPG sprawl.
- No meme or novelty content.
- No cluttered UI.
- No duplicate major systems.
- No forced grind or progression dead ends.

## Core Loop

Explore scenic terrain -> gather resources -> improve home and storage -> farm and cook -> decorate
and build infrastructure -> travel farther -> return with new blocks, food, memories, and project
ideas.

## Progression Shape

Early game:
- Gather resources peacefully.
- Build a small home.
- Explore nearby biomes, villages, and ruins.
- Establish starter farms, cooking, and storage.

Mid game:
- Expand farms, workshop, roads, bridges, and interiors.
- Improve base storage and mobile travel.
- Explore farther with maps, backpacks, waystones, and better food.
- Collect palettes for beautiful homes, barns, docks, gardens, and village streets.

Late game:
- Build impressive settlements and scenic projects.
- Connect villages and outposts through roads, bridges, and waystones.
- Use light comfort systems without turning the pack into a factory game.
- Keep exploring and building without progression collapse.

## Selection Rules

- Prefer Fabric 1.20.1.
- Prefer Modrinth/CurseForge verified sources.
- Prefer maintained, popular, stable mods.
- Choose complementary systems, not duplicate piles.
- Keep tech and automation light; do not include a major factory chain.
- Keep combat progression modest and avoid overpowered gear.
- Preserve the cozy survival loop as the dominant experience.

## Quality Bar

The final pack should feel like a professionally curated cozy exploration survival pack: beautiful
terrain, villages worth visiting, homes worth improving, satisfying food/storage/travel progression,
immersive ambience, and enough long-term goals to keep a solo world alive.

Do not call the pack stable unless launcher setup and runtime smoke testing pass.
