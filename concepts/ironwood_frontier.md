# Ironwood Frontier

## Overview

A cozy frontier survival and settlement-building modpack focused on chill
progression, beautiful wilderness exploration, farming, building, light machinery, and slowly
turning the wild frontier into a warm self-sufficient home.

The player settles scenic wild terrain: a simple cabin or camp becomes a homestead with farms,
kitchens, roads, bridges, barns, windmills or waterwheels, workshops, and storage—without heavy
factory tech, gun fantasy, dread-focused mob packs, or brutal survival realism.

## Core Fantasy

Explore rivers, forests, hills, caves, villages, and light frontier-flavored structures. Gather
materials, build warm wooden spaces, farm and cook better meals, lay paths and crossings, run small
Create-style comforts (mills, belts, scenic mechanical touches), and expand a settlement that stays
beautiful and livable long-term.

## Tone

- Warm wooden cabins and handcrafted infrastructure
- Forests, rivers, mountains, and open fields
- Cozy kitchens, farms, and animal life
- Soft ambience and a grounded soundscape
- Relaxed pacing with meaningful small goals

## Anti-Goals

- Heavy tech automation suites (Mekanism-scale, expert Create sprawl).
- Guns, explosives, infection-themed hostile overhauls, and dread-focused mob packs.
- Hardcore thirst/temperature realism or grindy survival gates.
- Kitchen sink stuffing, meme packs, or boss-heavy RPG bloat.

## Core Loop

Explore wilderness → gather resources → build homestead → farm and cook → improve storage and
travel → add simple mechanical comforts → expand settlement → explore farther for rare building
materials → keep beautifying the frontier.

## Progression Shape

**Early:** shelter, basic food, local exploration, starter storage.

**Mid:** farms and cooking palettes, richer building blocks, backpacks and maps, villages and light
structures, basic Create machinery for mills, waterwheels, and workshops; roads and settlement
layout.

**Late:** a large homestead or small town vibe, scenic farms and workshops, a comfortable travel
network, decoration depth, tasteful light automation, long-term building projects.

## Selection Rules

- Target Minecraft 1.20.1, Fabric.
- Prefer Modrinth; use CurseForge only through the official API when configured.
- Choose maintained, popular mods; avoid abandoned or unstable low-signal picks.
- Keep Create present but light (windmills, waterwheels, workshops, belts)—no heavy industrial addons.
- Shaders optional only; do not require shader packs.

## Quality Bar

The pack must feel cohesive: frontier homestead fantasy first, complementary mods second. Stability
claims require MythWeaver source resolution, dependency closure, verification, build/export,
launcher metadata validation, and runtime smoke testing—or an honest manual_required outcome.
