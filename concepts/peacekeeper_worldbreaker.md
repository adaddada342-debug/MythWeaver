# Peacekeeper / Worldbreaker

## Pack Identity

Peacekeeper / Worldbreaker is a Fabric 1.20.1 survival modpack about contrast. Most of the time
the world should feel calm, scenic, and worth caring for: a place to build a windmill, farm crops,
cook meals, improve villages, decorate cozy interiors, map distant valleys, and settle into a
beautiful long-term home.

The same world should also support an opt-in escalation path where the player becomes terrifyingly
powerful. Guns, supernatural-scale combat tools, and destructive options exist, but they are not the
default fantasy. They are a choice the player pursues through resources, crafting, exploration, and
late-game commitment.

Working title: Peacekeeper / Worldbreaker

Alternative title directions:
- Windmills & Warheads
- The Gentle Apocalypse
- Hearth & Havoc
- Bloom and Ruin
- The World You Spare
- Quiet Days, Loud Nights

## Core Fantasy

The player can live peacefully in a gorgeous overworld, building roads, farms, bridges, gardens,
barns, kitchens, and villages. They can decorate with care, cook real meals, travel between cozy
settlements, and make the world feel more human.

The player can also choose the opposite path: acquire serious ranged weapons, destructive tools, and
late-game power that can flatten hostile camps, overwhelm threats, and turn the same beautiful world
into a canvas for chaos.

The emotional hook is that both paths are valid. The world is beautiful enough to protect and
valuable enough that ruining it feels like a deliberate choice.

## Target Experience

1. Spawn in a scenic, relaxing overworld.
2. Explore villages, terrain, structures, animals, and quiet points of interest.
3. Gather resources and establish food, storage, and shelter.
4. Build a cozy home, farm, workshop, windmill, bridge network, or small settlement.
5. Improve villages with decoration, roads, lighting, kitchens, markets, and safe travel routes.
6. Discover stronger combat systems through crafting and exploration.
7. Decide how far to escalate into guns, destructive tools, and late-game power.
8. Protect settlements from danger or become the danger.
9. Continue through building goals, collection goals, exploration goals, and optional chaos goals.

## Design Pillars

1. Beautiful world worth caring about.
   The overworld should be scenic and peaceful, with strong terrain, improved villages, natural
   structures, wildlife, atmosphere, sound, and visual polish.

2. Cozy settlement building.
   The pack must strongly support farms, kitchens, windmills, interiors, bridges, roads, gardens,
   barns, village streets, and comfortable home bases.

3. Player choice between peace and destruction.
   The player should be able to ignore the chaos path and still have a full cozy survival pack, or
   pursue it and become absurdly dangerous.

4. High-quality power fantasy.
   Ranged weapons and late-game power systems should feel polished and survival-integrated. Avoid
   meme weapons, cheap novelty damage, and unconfigured instant godhood.

5. Controlled chaos.
   Destructive power should be opt-in and progression-gated. The first hour should be about
   settlement and survival, not immediate world deletion.

6. Performance and stability.
   Use a serious Fabric performance foundation. Avoid fragile worldgen stacking, boss-heavy bloat,
   and shader-dependent presentation.

## Explicit Anti-Goals

- No horror parasite, infection, stalker, or misery-survival focus.
- No brutal hardcore survival loops.
- No generic kitchen-sink mod pile.
- No excessive tech factory chains.
- No boss-heavy RPG bloat unless it directly supports power progression.
- No overlapping full terrain overhauls.
- No overlapping weapon systems fighting for attention.
- No low-quality gun mods, meme weapons, or random overpowered tools.
- No mods that make peaceful building annoying.
- No shader-only visual identity.

## Progression Shape

Early game:
- Find a scenic place to settle.
- Establish food, storage, shelter, and basic tools.
- Explore nearby villages and small structures.
- Begin decorating, farming, cooking, and road-building.

Mid game:
- Expand into proper settlement projects: windmill, barn, bridge, kitchen, market, gardens, and
  village improvements.
- Build a base storage system and mobile exploration kit.
- Discover the first serious ranged weapons or power components.
- Start crafting ammunition or resource-gated combat tools.

Late game:
- Choose a Peacekeeper path by protecting villages, building safe roads, improving settlements, and
  using power defensively.
- Choose a Worldbreaker path by investing in destructive weapons, dangerous combat tools, and large
  controlled explosions.
- Large destructive options should require rare materials, late-game crafting, exploration, or
  explicit configuration.

## Balance And Configuration Notes

- Guns should require crafted ammunition and should not trivialize the first day.
- Heavy weapons, explosives, and high-destruction tools should be rare, expensive, or disabled until
  a late-game phase.
- Include an SMP warning: destructive systems can grief builds unless claims/protection or social
  rules are used.
- Recommend optional claim/protection configuration for servers.
- Recommend a "peaceful builds protected" profile where destructive weapons are disabled near
  claimed settlements.
- If a mod exposes damage, explosion, or ammo configs, prefer conservative defaults.
- If a late-game power mod is very strong, document it as an opt-in Worldbreaker route rather than
  mandatory progression.

## Human Taste Review Targets

- Whether the gun system feels polished enough for the intended tone.
- Whether the late-game power route feels exciting without drowning out cozy survival.
- Whether village/settlement protection should be implemented through server claims, datapacks, or
  social rules.
- Whether the pack title should lean poetic, cozy, or violent.
