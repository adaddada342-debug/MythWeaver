A cozy, survival-first Minecraft modpack focused on building a beautiful world over time.

## Pack identity
- Core fantasy: settle into a gorgeous landscape, shape it into a lived-in home, and slowly grow a charming village-like settlement.
- Tone: warm, peaceful, and inviting.
- Difficulty: relaxed survival, low frustration, no hardcore punishment loops.
- Play style: solo-friendly with natural support for a small SMP of friends.

## Target experience
The player spawns in a scenic world and quickly finds inspiration for a home base. Early sessions focus on gathering basic materials, establishing food, and making a cozy starter house. Mid game expands into farming, cooking, animals, and decorative building projects. Long-term play is about beautification: paths, gardens, bridges, village improvements, interior design, and biome-specific outposts.

The world should constantly provide gentle goals:
- discover a beautiful biome
- collect new blocks and plants
- improve a house or village street
- add useful but charming infrastructure
- create a more atmospheric, polished environment

## Core loop
explore pretty terrain -> gather materials -> build/decorate -> farm/cook -> improve village/home -> unlock more building options -> make the world more beautiful

## Pillars (must dominate)
1. Cozy farming and homestead progression.
2. Building and decoration variety for exteriors and interiors.
3. Beautiful worldgen and relaxed exploration.
4. Villages, social spaces, and settlement improvement.
5. Atmosphere, visual polish, and comfort QoL.
6. Stable performance foundation for long play sessions.

- multiplayer_social

- creative_building_tools

## Explicit anti-goals
- no horror mods, stalker mobs, or jump-scare atmosphere
- no hardcore survival mechanics (extreme thirst, brutal temperature, punishing death loops)
- no combat-overhaul focus or overpowered gear escalation
- no boss-heavy progression as a central objective
- no grimdark or oppressive fantasy tone
- no heavy tech factories, long automation chains, or industry-first progression
- no magic systems that dominate progression
- no novelty meme mods that break immersion
- no kitchen-sink bloat or overlapping duplicate systems
- no systems that make building/decorating tedious

## Progression shape
- Early game: settle quickly, gather resources, establish food and storage, build a cozy starter home.
- Mid game: expand farms and kitchen, improve roads and landscaping, decorate interiors, upgrade village amenities.
- Late game: multi-biome aesthetic projects, themed districts, animal areas, scenic travel routes, and collaborative SMP beautification goals.

## Design constraints for selection
- Prefer maintained Fabric 1.20.1 mods unless a better fit is required.
- Keep total mod count realistic and curated.
- Every mod must have a clear purpose tied to the loop.
- Avoid including multiple mods that solve the same system unless they are known to pair well.
- Preserve survival gameplay while reducing stress and friction.
